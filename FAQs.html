FAQ

What is a Filter Press?

A Filter Press is essential for liquid/solid separation, using pressure filtration. It separates liquids and solids by pumping slurry into the press, where dewatering occurs under pressure. Our offerings include Sidebar Automatic, Manual Overhead, Automatic Dual Overhead Beam, Stainless Steel Clad, Explosion-proof Filter Presses, Vacuum Filter Presses, and Hand Filter Presses. What is an air-over-oil filter press?

An Air-Over-Oil Filter Press utilizes an air-assisted hydraulic cylinder for closing and clamping. It relies on an air-operated intensifier pump for pressure, with air directed to open the press. This is in contrast to Electro-Hydraulic powered cylinders. Learn more about hydraulic power pack solutions and troubleshooting.

What is the highest pressure I can pump to my filter press?

Filter presses are generally designed for pressures of 7 and 15 bar (100 and 225 psi). Special machines are available for higher pressures. Ensure not to exceed the designed pressure; refer to your equipment manual or contact us for assistance.

What is the best pump for a filter press?

Various pump types work well, with air diaphragm pumps being common due to their ability to handle slurries without shearing. Positive displacement pumps are suitable for high-pressure filters.

Can I easily replace my existing hydraulic power pack?

Yes, we manufacture modular power packs for air/oil and electro/hydraulic closure, easily installed in any brand of filter press. In some cases, an additional control panel may be required.

Is a filter press or centrifuge best suited to my dewatering/filtering application?

A Filter Press, like ours, is a mechanical liquid-solid separation machine using pressure, offering advantages in cake dryness and maintenance cost compared to centrifuges. Contact us to explore suitability for your application.

Can I wash filter cloths with a portable high-pressure washer?

Yes, using a fan-tipped hose at 800-900 psi, move it consistently over the cloth. If cleaning is inefficient, consider chemical removal of embedded particulates.

Can I chemically clean my filter cloth?

Chemical cleaning is effective for rejuvenating filtration performance. Polypropylene cloths accept caustic and acid cleaning; contact us for proper selection.

How do I extend my cloth life?

Cloth life is affected by mechanical damage and blinding. Record operating conditions and changes for evaluation and recommendations. Contact us for ways to extend filter cloth lifetime.

How long may I store filter cloth?

Synthetic cloths may be stored indefinitely; avoid UV exposure for polypropylene cloths. Contact us for specific recommendations.

Do you provide a micron rating for your filter cloths?

No, filter cloth specs are given in cubic feet per minute (CFM) permeability. Micron ratings are not provided; the filter cake becomes the true filter.

How can I tell if my filter cloth needs replacing?

Longer cycle times and progressively wetter filter cakes indicate the need for replacement. Contact us for assistance in selecting the right filter cloths.

Can I try samples to see if they work?

Yes, we provide a small number of filter cloth samples for initial evaluation. Contact us to request a sample and optimize your press operation.

How long do filter cloths last?

Filter cloth life varies based on factors like process, material, and maintenance. Observe signs like holes, folding, or poor cake release to determine replacement. Contact us for optimal performance.

Can I change the type of filter plate I have?

Yes, any filter plate type can be retrofitted to any filter press, adhering to the maximum operating pressure.

Why does my filter leak around the edges of the plates?

Edge leaking may result from specific slurry characteristics or excess back pressure. Poor filtrate discharge design, high pumping volumes, or incorrect filter cloths can contribute.

How can I buy spare filter press plates?

Provide correct dimensions through a questionnaire for custom-made plates. We manufacture plates for any brand, make, or model of filter press.

What is a replacement membrane?

A Replacement Membrane Plate has a replaceable membrane skin, offering cost-savings by keeping the original body plate. Learn more about Replacement Membranes here.

What are three-way filtrate valves?

Three-way Filtrate Valves isolate chambers, sample filtrate, or operate filter plates in closed delivery mode. Useful for locating filter cloth tears and isolating chambers.

What are one-button and three-button filter plates?

Proper filter plate sequencing is crucial for excellent press performance. One-button and three-button plates denote different porting for air blow or wash sequences. Ensure proper maintenance of sequencing for optimal performance.

What is open filtrate discharge?

Open Discharge involves external discharge to a trough, allowing easy visual identification of a failed filter cloth. Learn more about Recessed Filter Plate options here.

What is closed filtrate discharge?

Closed Discharge involves internal discharge to corner drain eyes, ideal for containing filtrate. Required for washing or air-drying filter cake. Explore Recessed Filter Plate options here.

Can I reduce my cycle time?

Normally, slurry characteristics impact cycle time. Conditioning the slurry and maintaining thinner cakes can improve dewatering time.
